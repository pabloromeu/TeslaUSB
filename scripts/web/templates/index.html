Storage & Retention

Controls how long archived dashcam clips are kept on the Pi's SD card and how aggressively the cleanup pass runs. Changes take effect on the next cleanup pass — no service restart needed.

Heads up: short retention may delete footage of incidents that take time to discover (e.g., scratches noticed days later).

Applied to ArchivedClips and any folder without an override below.

After the time-based pass, if SD-card free space drops below this percentage, the oldest archived clips are deleted (oldest first) until free space crosses back above the target. Cloud-pending clips are kept; disk image files are protected. Set to 0 to disable.

Hard cap on total archive size. After the time-based pass, if total .mp4 bytes under the archive root exceed this cap, oldest clips are deleted until total is at or below the cap. Cloud-pending clips are kept; disk image files are protected. Leave at 0 to use only time-based retention.

Show the yellow warning banner when default retention drops below this.

Per-folder overrides (advanced)

Override retention for individual Tesla folders. Leave a row disabled to inherit the default. ArchivedClips lives on the Pi's SD card; the others (SentryClips, SavedClips, RecentClips, EncryptedClips) are inside the USB image that Tesla writes to.

Reclaim space from stationary RecentClips

Deletes archived RecentClips with no GPS movement (parked Sentry-mode footage, no driving, no events). Saved/Sentry event clips are preserved even if a RecentClips copy of the same recording exists. This can free a lot of space — on a typical install, RecentClips dominates the SD card.

Stationary RecentClips skipped (last 24h)

The archive worker peeks at each new RecentClips file and skips copying it to the SD card if no GPS movement is detected — this keeps overnight Sentry-mode parking footage from filling the SD card. Sentry/Saved event clips are never skipped.

Archive Settings

Tesla records dashcam footage to the USB drive in a circular buffer called RecentClips. After about an hour, older clips are automatically overwritten by new ones. The archive service copies these clips to the Pi's SD card before they're lost, so you can browse and cloud-sync them later.

When enabled, clips are automatically copied from the USB drive to the SD card every 2 minutes. Disable this if you don't need local copies of your dashcam footage.

Archived clips older than this are automatically deleted to free up SD card space.

Archival stops when SD card free space drops below this. Protects the OS and other services from running out of disk.

Mapping & Indexing

Tesla embeds GPS coordinates and telemetry data (speed, braking, steering) inside each dashcam video. The indexer extracts this data and builds a database of trips, routes, and driving events. This powers the map view — showing where you drove, plotting routes, and detecting events like hard braking or sharp turns.

Scans dashcam videos to extract GPS waypoints and build trip routes. When disabled, the map page won't show any trips or routes. Videos can still be browsed by folder.
